Site Update 09/23

Feature Changes

  • User bios now display at top of page on mobile. Before, the user bio/sidebar on mobile was only accessible if the user clicked the button in the navbar to reveal it. Now a minimized (and expandable) version of the user bio will be shown at the top of a user's blog on mobile for ease of access. The display of the user bio is unchanged (i.e. still a sidebar) on desktop.
  • Comment threads will now only load the first 5 levels of comments automatically, with a button to load more comments. This was done in part to fix an ongoing bug where comment threads couldn't be expanded beyond 20 nested levels, as well as to cut down on visual clutter on busy comment threads.

Tweaks & Bug Fixes

  • Photos uploaded from mobile will now retain the proper rotation data.
  • Fixed styling problems with the "submit" button and loading spinner display when submitting a new post or reblog.
  • An erroneous error that a post's comments have been locked will no longer show while a post's data is being loaded in the post view modal.
  • Fixed a bug where locking a post's comments sometimes still allowed replies to be submitted to existing comments.
  • Users will no longer be able to queue or schedule posts to communities (this functionality has never been available, but users will now no longer be able to click to queue a post to a community which will then disappear into the aether.)

For the next release we're working on the long-awaited revamp of the image grid uploader, and our post promotion feature. Thanks for your patience as we've been working on these things, and stay tuned for more updates!

- Staff

Regarding NSFW content & payment processors

Hi all, we want to address some concerns that have come up in our community as a result of recent events surrounding NSFW content, payment processors, and age verification. It's clear a lot is changing recently concerning access to adult content on the web.

First, regarding the removal/delisting of "adult content" by Steam and itch.io recently: we do not expect that Pillowfort will be affected by this. For one thing, we do not sell access to adult content, so the rules that have caused Steam & itch to make these decisions don't apply for us as we are not selling games or other content. 

However, even without selling adult content, simply having adult content on one's site can create issues with payment processors who don't allow clients to even host that kind of content. You don't have to worry about this regarding Pillowfort, because we have already dealt with that issue. Early PF adoptees may remember that we used to accept payments through PayPal, but Paypal closed our account without warning years ago simply because we allow our users to upload adult content. As such, we have to use a payment processor that specifically handles "high risk" clients such as adult content-related businesses. This comes with much higher processing fees than "regular" payment processors to compensate for the "risk," which adds to our financial challenges. The benefit is though, that unless something drastically changes, we should be safe from payment processor censorship. 

However, if you are mad about these recent developments, we greatly encourage you to let Visa/Mastercard know! It would be a huge game changer for us if services like PayPal and Stripe could be pressured into relaxing their content restrictions for their clients.

Secondly, we have been asked about the recent legislation enacted in the UK that requires sites that host adult content to require age verification, even those hosted outside the UK. This is a hairier issue since the guidelines are very loosely-defined in places, and frankly we do not yet know how this will affect us, but we are reaching out to sources that can help us navigate this. We hate the idea of forcing our UK users to submit to privacy-violating age verification, but we cannot deny that it may simply be necessary for us to implement it to avoid fines and litigation. We will let you all know if any changes will be coming as we explore our options.

Thank you all for your concern and for supporting Pillowfort through all this. It's a turbulent time on the web, and a reminder of why it is necessary for independent spaces to exist that protect the creative freedom of their users.

Best,
Staff

Hi all, just a little update to let you know what our team has been working on!

  • We're working on a site update that will address a handful of long-standing issues, such as mobile images being rotated incorrectly when uploaded. Premium Prices & 10MB upload limit!

Hey everyone! We've updated Pillowfort Premium's pricing. The prices are now:

Upload Limit Increase (6MB):  $3.49

Multiple Avatars: $2.49

The cost of avatar frames is staying the same. If you have an existing subscription and aren't using credit, you will pay the new subscription amount the next time your subscription is charged. You can see how much that amount will be on the Subscriptions page. If you are using credits to cover part or all of the cost of your premium features, the cost of your subscription will not change until your credited payments end. This is because the payment processor we use doesn't allow us to change existing subscriptions that are using "trial" periods, which is how we handle credited payments. Consider it an extra gift from us! ;)

Announcement: Pillowfort Premium Price Increase 2025

Hi everyone,

To help Pillowfort remain sustainable for the long term, we will be increasing our Pillowfort Premium Subscription fees on July 1st, 2025.

Upload Limit Increase (6MB): 
$2.49 increased to  $3.49

Multiple Avatars: 
$1.99 increased to $2.49

These price increases will be automatically applied to all existing subscriptions on July 1st. If you are subscribed and using credits to cover the cost of features, the price increase will not affect how many payments your credits will cover, i.e. you will not have to pay the new pricing until your credited payments run out.

Why are we raising prices?

We want Pillowfort to remain online for many years to come.  The pricing adjustments take into consideration previous community-wide survey results that showed overwhelming support to raise prices within a certain range.
